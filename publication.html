Early grade reading outcomes in multilingual classrooms: A longitudinal study across six provinces

REF · RES · 014  ·  Dr. N. Mthembu  ·  Published 2023  ·  Zenex Foundation

This is the same publication, one URL — the tabs change what's emphasised, not what exists.

Executive summary

This report presents findings from a three-year longitudinal study examining reading outcomes in Foundation Phase classrooms where instruction occurs in a home language other than English or Afrikaans. The study tracked learner progress across Grades 1–3 in 48 schools across six provinces, and finds that structured literacy approaches significantly improve Grade 3 reading comprehension when teachers receive adequate professional development support.

What this means for your province

Districts that paired structured home-language literacy approaches with sustained teacher professional development saw measurably stronger Grade 3 comprehension gains than districts relying on materials alone. For provincial planning, the sequencing — PD support alongside rollout, not as a once-off — is what distinguishes districts that closed the gap from those that didn't.

Key findings

Across the six participating provinces, classrooms using structured home-language literacy instruction showed consistent gains in decoding and comprehension over the three-year window, most pronounced where teachers received ongoing professional development rather than a single training session. Classrooms that adopted materials without the accompanying PD support showed smaller, though still positive, gains — suggesting the two interventions reinforce each other rather than substitute for one another.

Programme & partnership fit

This study builds directly on Zenex's Foundation Phase literacy portfolio and the LEAD evaluation capacity-building programme. Organisations already working in Foundation Phase literacy, particularly in multilingual and African-language classroom contexts, are best positioned to extend this work at district level.

Evaluation design & governance

Findings draw on a three-year longitudinal design with learner-level tracking across Grades 1–3, independently reviewed as part of Zenex's standing M&E discipline. Full evaluation protocol, sampling method and limitations are documented for scrutiny — consistent with Zenex's commitment to evidence discipline across all commissioned research.

Abstract & methodology

Full methodology: three-year longitudinal tracking of learner progress across Grades 1–3 in 48 schools across six provinces, comparing classrooms with structured home-language literacy instruction against standard practice. Data collection combined reading assessments at three annual time points with classroom observation of teacher practice. Analysis controls for baseline performance, school socio-economic quintile, and language of learning and teaching.
